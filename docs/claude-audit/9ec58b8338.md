### 🔴 HIGH ISSUES

**H1 — SVG polyline points render at edge, clipping the stroke (AC2, AC3)**
`web/src/components/progress_sparkline.rs:49`

The Y coordinate maps data directly to 0.0 and 24.0 — the very edges of the viewBox. With `stroke-width="1.5"`, the top and bottom peaks get clipped by 0.75px. The iOS equivalent uses an inset (padding) to keep the stroke within bounds. The sparkline should use a small margin (e.g. y maps to range 1–23 instead of 0–24) to prevent clipping.

**H2 — `ewma_str` is cloned into two owners but Leptos closure borrows it twice (AC5)**
`web/src/components/progress_sparkline.rs:80-84`

`ewma_str` is used in both `aria` (line 84) and the `<span>` (line 105). Since `ewma_str` is moved into the `format!` for `aria`, and then used again in the view macro — this works because `view!` captures the remaining string at the same scope level, but it means the EWMA text appears both in the visible `<span>` AND in the `aria-label`. A screen reader will announce the EWMA twice: once from `aria-label` on the container, and again from the visible text child. The `aria-label` **overrides** the accessible name of child content, so this isn't a double-announcement in the traditional sense, but the container's `aria-label` prevents the inner text from being read naturally if a user navigates into the container. This is actually correct behavior for AC10 — downgrading to informational. **REVISED: Not a bug, aria-label correctly overrides children.**
